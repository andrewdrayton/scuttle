## Positioning Statement

"Generalist operator at a founder-led company where the chaos is creative and operational, not analytical. The GODA experience is a differentiator in rooms where that kind of operator is rare, not in rooms that want a strategist who can also execute."

Use this as the north star for scoring and filtering. If a role's primary need is a strategist who executes, or an analyst who also ships, it is not a fit regardless of title or industry. If the role needs someone who builds operating infrastructure inside a creative or cultural org where things are moving fast and nobody has laid the foundation yet — that is the exact seat.

## Track System

Track 1: Culturally significant operator roles at founder-led companies in target industries. Real scope, real seat.

Track 2: Real operator roles at companies that don't perfectly match Track 1 cultural profile but are legitimate fits. Mid-size DTC, e-commerce, services. Operations Manager or Director titles at reputable companies.

Track 2B: Roles that meet the profile loosely but have meaningful gaps (industry mismatch, scope unclear, level mismatch).

## Honest Credential Calibration

Score "Credential Match" against what a sophisticated recruiter at the target company would think, not against the title on the resume. Specifically:

- COO at small startup ≠ COO at growth-stage company. Do not treat GODA experience as equivalent to having scaled ops at a 100+ person org.
- Title duration ≠ scope. 3 years as COO at GODA reflects 3 years of small-team operating, not 3 years of enterprise ops leadership.
- Prior to GODA: Aramark was project coordination, not operations leadership. Film production was logistics, not strategy.

For roles at large established companies (Anthropic, Google, Netflix, Nike, Meta), default credential match to 3-5/10 unless the role explicitly values 0-to-1 startup builders or cultural credibility over traditional enterprise scaling experience. If the JD contains "0-to-1," "founder-mode," "build from scratch," or similar language, the JD overrides the company-size default and credential match can rise to 6-7/10.

For roles requiring specific functional experience Andrew doesn't have (partner ops, RevOps, supply chain, finance ops, people ops), credential match should be 2-4/10 regardless of seniority.

For roles at early-stage founder-led companies in target industries (creative, apparel, media), credential match can be 6-8/10 because the GODA experience is genuinely relevant to that context.

Cultural credibility bonus: KAWS, Pharrell, Shaun Neff, and the broader GODA co-founder network carry real weight in talent agency portfolio companies, streetwear brands, and culture-adjacent orgs. At those companies, bump credential match up 1-2 points above the baseline — this is a real differentiator that standard recruiter screening won't surface but matters in those rooms.

## Learned Filters & Location Preferences

### Anthropic Filter

Do not blanket-skip Anthropic. Apply this filter instead.

Surface Anthropic roles only if the JD explicitly mentions one or more of:
- 0-to-1 build or standing up a new function from scratch
- Cross-functional or creative stakeholder management
- Early-stage program with no existing infrastructure
- Cultural or brand-adjacent mandate
- Community, partnerships, or external-facing operations

Auto-skip Anthropic roles that are primarily:
- RevOps, sales ops, revenue strategy, or go-to-market systems
- Salesforce, data infrastructure, or technical systems ownership
- Recruiting, HR, or people ops function
- Engineering or security program management
- International-only locations (Sydney, Tokyo, etc.)
- "Specialist" or "Coordinator" in the title regardless of framing

Calibration note: COO at a 5-person creative venture is not equivalent to ops leadership at a 100+ person company. For Anthropic roles, default Credential Match to 4-6/10 unless the JD explicitly calls out 0-to-1 build experience or cultural operator background as a requirement. The Community Operations & Executive Escalations Manager role from Session 1 is the right benchmark for what clears the bar.

### Relocation Preferences

Domestic relocation cities: New York City, San Francisco

Surface any role in NYC or SF at Director level or above (Director, VP, Head of, GM, COO, Chief of Staff). Do not surface coordinator, specialist, or manager-without-team roles in these cities as relocation targets.

Scoring:
- Role explicitly mentions relocation assistance: flag with note "relocation assistance mentioned", score normally
- Role is Director-level or above with no relocation mention: flag with note "relocation likely negotiable at this level", score normally
- Role is below Director level with no relocation mention: apply standard location filter, do not surface as relocation candidate

International relocation cities: London, Milan

These require visa sponsorship. Surface roles in London or Milan only if:
1. The JD explicitly mentions visa sponsorship or right-to-work assistance, OR
2. The role is Director-level or above AND the profile warrants sponsorship (COO/co-founder background, cultural industry experience, named collaborators)

For condition 2 use this as a guideline: a hiring manager at a culturally credible brand in London or Milan looking for a senior ops leader with GODA/Pharrell/Neff credentials would realistically consider sponsoring. A coordinator role would not.

Scoring:
- Visa sponsorship explicitly mentioned: flag "visa sponsorship mentioned", score normally
- Senior role where sponsorship is realistic: flag "visa sponsorship not mentioned, likely negotiable at this level", reduce Fit score by 1 point to reflect uncertainty
- Role below Director level or sponsorship clearly unrealistic: do not surface

Hard exclusion cities remain: Boston. Do not surface Boston roles regardless of level or relocation assistance.

Remote roles: Continue to prioritize. Remote roles with no location requirement score normally with no relocation flag needed.
